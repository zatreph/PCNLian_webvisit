PCNLians triple in number within 5 years

For the last 5 years Philippine College of Northwestern Luzon has experienced and immense growth in the population. From 433 pc and alien in the 2020 tripling of this year to 1,294. PCNL's population has gradually increased. Such growth is truly observed last a. y. 2023 - 2024 , when the number of the freshmen or new enroll is went almost half of the total population (527 out of 1098). The rising number is seen primarily as the result of the accessible and affordable quality education the school delivers and numerous school promotional videos release online. Consistently, the majority belongs to the Criminology Department, followed by Information Technology, Education, Business Administration and Accountancy Departments respectively. The passing rate on the board programs the institutions offers; BSA, 100% in may 2022; BSCrim, 66.7% in the July 2022; BSA,66.67% in the March 2024; is a contributing factor to such growth in the population. The academe's ability to mold and produce passers truly motivates secondary graduates to enroll at PCNL and be just like them in the future. The efforts of the admins staff and the faculty in doing career guidance in various secondary institution as well as their credentials and stories are also seen as a strong forces pulling in new enrollees. As time goes by this rising data of PCNLian is expected to grow even higher specially when the new majors BSE Major in Mathematics and BSBA Major in Marketing and the Engineering program are made available to enrollees. Soon in time PCNL will be known to many more not only as one of the few institutions in the Agoo but as a institution of merit and growth serving "qualitas et excellentiam"

PCNL staffers engage in seminar-workshop

In preparation of the upcoming sports fest and other school events, PCNL coordinated with DMMMSU-MLUC's THE MLUC VOICE advisers and staffers in holding a 1-day seminar workshop for PCNL Express News (PEN) journalists last October 14, 2024. Speakers of the MLUC VOICE headed by Mrs. Maria Evelyn Corpuz and Mr. Rogelio Joshua Catbagan together with three staffers (former and present), extended their expertise in journalism, sharpening the skills and broadening the capabilities of the PEN staffers. Mr. Catbagan focused on news writing and feature writing, Mr JD Magcanta talks about sports writing Ms. Hannah Morales assisted in photojournalism and Mr. Mar Lou Alcantara discussed about the opinion and editorial writing. They all provided tips and structure guidelines the staffers may use to further enhance their journalistic ability achieving the theme of the event: "Writing with Impact: Mastering Article Writing and Digital Media"

SSC Clean-Up Dry

A greener, cleaner, and safer PCNL is what we have now after the success of the Clean-up Drive, led by the SSC officers last September 14, 2024. Countless hands participated for the beautification of the institutions' environment and safeguarding the health of PCNLians through clean surroundings. Initiated by the SSC officers, they were accompanied by various departmental officers, two school organization advisers, Mr. Erwin Ching of SSC and Ms. Marian Jomalyn Aquino of PEN, and NSTP-CWTS Instructors, Mr. Antonio Bustamante, Mr. John Geneta and Mr. Rey Rosido. NSTP-CWTS students have also lend their hands in making the goal possible. NSTP-ROTC students also came to help on the said activity after their formation at DMMMSU-SLUC. This initiative is just the beginning of the numerous student and institution-centered programs of the SSC. May we all continue to be with them in creating a better learning environment and a safer home to all PCNLians.
