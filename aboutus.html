HISTORY

The Philippine College of Northern Luzon was established with the goal of offering accessible education to the region.

VISION

To be a distinctive learning institution acknowledged far and wide for its innovative and flexible approaches in working with its students, stakeholders, and community to create a more just and human society and a more sustainable economy.

MISSION

In support of its philosophy, PCNL mandates itself to provide advanced quality instruction in education, the arts and humanities, science, technology, business, and other professional fields; to promote research; and to improve quality of life through extension work.

PHILOSOPHY

PCNL believes in holistic education that nurtures not only intellectual capacity but also values, creativity, and community service, empowering students to contribute meaningfully to society.

INSTITUTIONAL PROFILE OF PHILIPPINE COLLEGE OF NORTHWESTERN LUZON HISTORY The demand for globalization requires the development of highly-skilled and fully educated human resources to meet the ever changing challenges for achieving competitive labor force. A Higher Educational Institution (HEI) was established after a survey was conducted by way of random sampling of parents from La Union and from the north and Western towns of Pangasinan, whose children are in elementary and secondary schools. After the organizational meeting of the incorporators, the organization came up with the name Philippine College of Northwestern Luzon (PCNL) with articles of incorporation and by-laws duly commission, approved Baguio by the Extension Securities Office on andtheExchange 20th day of February 2007. All CHED requirements were prepared by the group including the province prospective courses to be offered namely Bachelor of Science in Business Administration (BSBA), Bachelor of Science in Accountancy (BSA), Bachelor of Science in Secondary Elementary Education (BEE),and (BSE), Bachelor of Science in Information Technology (BSIT). While the institution is waiting for the approval of Commission on Higher Education (CHED), the Board of Trustees composed of 9 members of the 15 incorporators likewise preferred the Technical Education and Skills Development authority (TESDA) requirements of offering short term courses. After the visit of TESDA technical panel, the institution was granted a permit to offer four short term courses namely: Computer Hardware Servicing NC 11, Computer 7 Programming NC 1V, PC Operation NC 11, and Tourism Housekeeping NC 11 which started the 1st semester of the school year 2007-2008. On February 2008, the courses offered by CHED are BSBA and BSA to be offered during the first semester of school year 2008-2009.The following year, BSE and BEE where approved followed by BSIT and CRIMINOLOGY respectively. PCNL is located in an environment conducive to learning and development. There are no manufacturing and industrial establishments near the school and science laboratories.
